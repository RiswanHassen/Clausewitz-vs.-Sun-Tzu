# Clausewitz vs. Sun Tzu

**A Strategic Essay on the Bundeswehr's Doctrinal Blind Spots**

This repository contains a strategic essay examining the German Bundeswehr's relationship with military tradition (*Traditionspflege*), Clausewitz's blind spot regarding deception and *Kriegslist*, and why Sun Tzu's thinking is more relevant than ever in the age of hybrid warfare.

---

## Thesis

The Bundeswehr's doctrinal foundation leans heavily on Clausewitzian thinking — war as the continuation of politics, the decisive battle, the primacy of will. This framework served conventional interstate conflict well. It is inadequate for the conflicts Germany actually faces: hybrid warfare, information operations, grey-zone coercion, and asymmetric threats that blur the line between peace and war.

Sun Tzu's emphasis on deception, indirect approach, and winning without fighting offers a corrective — not as a replacement, but as a necessary complement. The essay argues that the Bundeswehr's reluctance to engage with *Kriegslist* is not just a doctrinal gap but a cultural one, rooted in post-war identity constraints that deserve critical examination.

## Contents

The essay is written in German and addresses:

- The historical trajectory of *Traditionspflege* in the Bundeswehr
- Clausewitz's treatment (and dismissal) of deception in *Vom Kriege*
- Sun Tzu's relevance to modern hybrid and asymmetric conflict
- The intersection with international law (*Völkerrecht*) and proportionality
- Practical implications for doctrine, training, and strategic culture

## Format

Markdown essay, intended for discussion and reference. Not an academic paper — a practitioner's argument.
